<p><p>The systemic psoriasis therapeutics market primarily includes biologics and small molecules. Biologics are advanced therapies derived from living organisms, targeting specific immune pathways, and are effective for moderate to severe psoriasis. They often require subcutaneous injection or intravenous administration. Small molecules, on the other hand, are orally administered drugs that modulate immune responses and inflammation. Both types aim to alleviate symptoms, improve quality of life, and reduce the long-term effects of psoriasis, catering to diverse patient needs in treatment approaches.</p></p>
