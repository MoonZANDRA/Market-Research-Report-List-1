<p><p>The phosphorescent colorants market is divided into two main types: organic and inorganic phosphorescent colorants. Organic phosphorescent colorants are primarily carbon-based compounds that exhibit long-lasting luminescence, making them suitable for applications in consumer goods, textiles, and packaging. In contrast, inorganic phosphorescent colorants are typically metal-based materials, known for their durability and stability, often used in industrial applications, coatings, and safety signage. Both types are valued for their ability to absorb light and re-emit it over extended periods, enhancing visibility and aesthetics.</p></p>
