<p><p>The uncooled infrared sensors market primarily consists of two types: thermal and quantum sensors. Thermal sensors, such as microbolometers, detect infrared radiation by sensing temperature changes, making them suitable for various applications like building temperature monitoring and surveillance. In contrast, quantum sensors, including photon detectors and photodiodes, leverage quantum mechanics to achieve higher sensitivity and accuracy, excelling in applications like gas detection and medical imaging. Together, these sensor types cater to diverse industries, enhancing thermal imaging capabilities.</p></p>
