<p><p>The automotive fuel additives market is primarily divided into two categories: gasoline additives and diesel additives. Gasoline additives enhance fuel performance by improving combustion efficiency, reducing emissions, and preventing engine deposits. They often include detergents, octane boosters, and anti-knock agents. Diesel additives, on the other hand, focus on enhancing cetane numbers, reducing smoke emissions, and preventing fuel wax formation in cold temperatures. Both types of additives contribute to better engine performance, efficiency, and longevity, catering to the specific needs of different fuel types.</p></p>
