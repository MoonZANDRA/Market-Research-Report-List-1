<p><p>Vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty devices are minimally invasive surgical tools used to treat vertebral compression fractures, commonly caused by osteoporosis or trauma. These procedures involve the injection of bone cement into the fractured vertebra to stabilize the structure and reduce pain. While vertebroplasty is primarily focused on stabilizing the fracture, kyphoplasty additionally aims to restore vertebral height by inflating a balloon before cement injection.</p><p>The market for vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty devices is witnessing robust growth, driven by an increasing aging population, rising prevalence of osteoporosis, and technological advancements in minimally invasive surgical techniques.
