<p><p>Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene (ABS) resin is produced through various polymerization methods, primarily emulsion graft copolymerization and bulk copolymerization. Emulsion graft copolymerization involves polymerizing monomers in an aqueous medium, resulting in smaller particle sizes and enhanced impact resistance. In contrast, bulk copolymerization occurs in a solid or liquid phase, allowing for higher yields and better control over the molecular structure. Both methods contribute significantly to the ABS market by catering to diverse applications, from automotive components to consumer goods.</p></p>
