<p><p>The dried herbs market is segmented into two primary types: whole herbs and powdered herbs. Whole herbs are intact leaves, flowers, or seeds that retain their natural essence and flavors, making them ideal for culinary uses and infusions. On the other hand, powdered herbs are finely ground, allowing for easier incorporation into recipes and quicker flavor release. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences, with whole herbs favored for their aromatic qualities and powdered herbs for convenience and versatility in cooking.</p></p>
